The Sacred Vitthal Rukmini Mandir

Vitthal Rukmini Mandir is the main temple in Pandharpur, dedicated to Lord Vitthal (a form of Lord Krishna) and his consort Rukmini. The temple is considered one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in Maharashtra and attracts millions of devotees every year.

The temple has a rich history dating back to the 13th century and is closely associated with the Bhakti movement and saints like Dnyaneshwar and Tukaram. The temple's architecture showcases the Hemadpanthi style with influences from North Indian temple architecture.

About Pandharpur

Pandharpur is a holy city situated on the banks of the Chandrabhaga River in Solapur district of Maharashtra. Known as the spiritual capital of Maharashtra, Pandharpur is famous for the Vitthal Rukmini Temple and the Wari pilgrimage that takes place every year.

The city has been a center of spiritual learning and devotion for centuries, attracting saints, philosophers, and devotees from all over India. The Bhakti movement in Maharashtra was centered around Pandharpur, making it an important cultural and religious hub.
